CS1332: Data Structures and Algorithms

This was my second computer science class at Georgia Tech and perhaps one of my favorite classes. It provided a strong foundation to understand and implement various data structure in Java 7 such as: Arrays, Linked List, Stacks, Priority Queues, Heaps, HashSets and HashMaps. Additionally, the class familiarized me with determining the time and space complexity of algorithms. I used this knowledge to implement various algorithms such as: KMP, Boyer-Moore, Rabin-Karp, DFS, BFS, Krushal's, Dijsktra's, Insertion Sort, Selection Sort, Merge Sort, and Quick Sort.
